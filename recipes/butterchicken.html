Indian Butter Chicken

About Indian Butter Chicken

Makhani Chicken, often called Indian Butter Chicken, is a beloved curry known for its rich, creamy tomato sauce and tender, marinated chicken. Originating from Indian cuisine, this dish features a fragrant blend of spices, yogurt-marinated chicken, and a luxurious makhani (butter) gravy that pairs perfectly with basmati rice or naan bread.

Ingredients

    For the Marinade:

  • 1 ¾ pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast, cubed
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice, divided
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder, divided
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons ginger paste
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 ½ teaspoons garam masala

    • For the Sauce:

  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 1 tablespoon chopped garlic
  • 1 tablespoon chopped green chile pepper
  • 2 cups tomato puree
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • ½ teaspoon dried fenugreek leaves
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Step-By-Step Guide

  1. Marinate the Chicken

    In a bowl, combine the cubed chicken with 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, 1 tablespoon of chili powder, and salt. Mix well to coat. Cover and refrigerate for about 1 hour to let the flavors penetrate the chicken.

  2. Prepare Yogurt Mixture

    Line a strainer with cheesecloth and place the plain yogurt inside to drain excess liquid for 15–20 minutes. Transfer the drained yogurt to a bowl and stir in the remaining lemon juice, olive oil, garlic paste, ginger paste, melted butter, remaining chili powder, and garam masala.

  3. Second Marination

    Pour this yogurt spice mixture over the chicken, mix thoroughly, and refrigerate again for 3–4 hours. This extended marination step helps tenderize the chicken and deepen the flavor.

  4. Cook the Chicken

    Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto skewers and arrange them in a baking dish. Bake until the chicken is almost cooked through, about 20 minutes.

  5. Make the Sauce

    In a saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter and add the garam masala. Once fragrant, stir in the ginger paste, garlic, and green chile pepper. Sauté briefly until aromatic. Add the tomato puree, water, chili powder, and salt. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer. Stir in the honey and dried fenugreek leaves.

  6. Combine Chicken and Sauce

    Remove the par-cooked chicken from its skewers and add it to the simmering sauce. Continue cooking until the chicken is fully cooked and no longer pink in the center (internal temperature should reach 165°F/74°C).

  7. Finish with Cream

    Stir in the heavy cream until it is fully incorporated, creating a smooth, velvety sauce. Remove from heat and let the curry rest briefly before serving.
